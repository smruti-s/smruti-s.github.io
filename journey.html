About Me

From a young age, I have been captivated by machines and instruments. This curiosity led to becoming my fatherâ€™s assistant for home repairs. I loved tinkering with the various mechanics, electronics, and robotics kits I received as birthday gifts. Designing and building a proof-of-concept aqua robot for disaster relief in 8th grade helped me appreciate the positive impact robotics could have on the world. My passion for pursuing an education and career in robotics stems from a deep-seated desire to use technology to help people in need, particularly in aid and rescue scenarios.

My undergraduate studies in Electronics and Instrumentation was structured to build a strong foundation in robotics. My continued interest in Mathematics and Computation, along with courses like Control Systems, Data Structures & Algorithms, and Microcontroller based system design enabled me to develop vital skills in control design, programming, logic-forming, and problem-solving.

I recently graduated with a Master of Science in Robotics and a graduate certificate in Engineering Leadership from Northeastern University, Boston.
