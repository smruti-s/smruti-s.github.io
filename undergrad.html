Undergraduate Research Experience

  1. Analysis and consolidated results of particle flow through a microfluidic channel of an RBC particle:

    • Analyzed particles' flow through the channel by varying the microfluidic channel and dimensions in both 2D and 3D workspace in the COMSOL software.
    • Analyzed variation in particle movement when a hollow pillar was inserted in the microfluidic channel.
    • Traced out the particle movement in laminar flow in the 3D workspace.

  2. Design and Development of an Instrumentation System for "Taila Bindu Pariksha"- An Ayurvedic diagnostic system:

    • Conducted an in-depth literature review to gain a concrete understanding of the Taila Bindu Pariksha diagnostic system and the underlying physics principles governing the formation of sesame oil shapes in urine.
    • Established a comprehensive database comprising various shapes produced by sesame oil in urine samples from individuals with different diseases to implement pattern matching and disease diagnosis.
    • Led the image processing component of the project, leveraging MATLAB to implement advanced image analysis techniques

  3. Impact of illumination and its impact on exact face boundary detection:

    • Analyzed the impact of illumination on real-time face detection using Open CV, Python and Deep Learning to come up with methods to reduce its adverse effect on the exact boundary detection of the human face and its features.
